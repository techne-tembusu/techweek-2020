About

TechWeek is a week-long initiative by Techne that aims to encourage discussion on the relevance of technology in today's society, and how it can be sensibly utilized for the greater good.

Speaker Profile

Eyung is the Director of the Digital Design & Development (DCube) team at the Government Digital Services (GDS) division in GovTech. His key roles include communicating with other government agencies to understand the issues faced by them and proposing how the various functional and cross-functional teams of GDS can come together to help. Within his team, he works with different team leads and oversees the delivery of products to the agencies.


Company Profile

Government Technology Agency of Singapore (GovTech) drives digital transformation within the public sector and harnesses the best technologies to make a difference to the everyday lives of Singaporeans. Specifically, DCube’s approach is guided by Agile and User Experience Design (UXD) principles to ensure that product development is conducted in a manner that is user-centric and iterative with continuous improvements. The digital services developed as an outcome of cross-team collaboration creates great impact to our society by bringing value to Singaporeans.

Speaker Profile

Prof Dean Ho is Provost’s Chair Professor, Director of The N.1 Institute for Health (N.1), Director of the Institute for Digital Medicine (WisDM), and Head of the Department of Biomedical Engineering at the National University of Singapore. Using his CURATE.AI platform, Prof Ho has led multiple pioneering clinical studies that have validated the promise of N-of-1 medicine, where only a patient’s own data is used to personalise their treatment for the entire duration of care. Multiple CURATE.AI-based clinical studies are ongoing or cleared for start in the areas of solid cancer and blood cancer therapy, digital therapeutics/personalised learning, and post-organ transplant immunosuppression, among others.


Company Profile

IDentif.AI harnesses the use of AI and Digital Medicine to rapidly optimise combination therapy design against infectious diseases. Unlike traditional AI methods. IDentif.AI does not use pre-existing data for algorithm training and predictions. Instead, it pairs prospective experimentation and optimisation to realise a data-backed and ranked list of combination therapy regimens for clinician selection.
